The solution presented attempts to address the crucial problem of identifying and alleviating negative emotions among Sri Lanka's middle-class population. Our novel method makes use of machine learning and unsupervised learning techniques. Composed of four parts, the first three are text, audio, or video classification to analyze a person's emotional state. The fourth component is a recommendation system offering personalized recommendations to help users alleviate themselves from the negative emotion faced. The middle-class population in Sri Lanka is increasingly concerned about the impact the country's economic crisis and difficult living conditions may have on their physical and mental health as they work to support their families daily. Understanding how urgent it is to solve this urgent problem; our approach provides useful techniques to reduce and lessen these emotional loads as well as early identification of negative emotions making it easier to alleviate their mental state. A happier and more productive group of people is what this all-encompassing approach promises to bring about. Our system's scalability and adaptability to different situations also enable customers to adapt it to suit their own requirements and preferences.

Text-based Emotion Classification

Aims to enhance the performance of emotion classification leveraging a novel data augmentation technique specialized for classification tasks.

Audio-based Emotion Classification

Enhances emotion classification accuracy through advanced preprocessing and data augmentation, focusing on extracting robust spectral and temporal features from audio signals.

Image-based Emotion Classification

Enhances emotion detection by using a CNN model with attention mechanisms to improve generalization in challenging visual conditions.

LITERATURE SURVEY

In text classification, data augmentation is vital in Natural Language Processing (NLP), especially for low-resource languages with scarce annotated datasets. Techniques like Easy Data Augmentation (EDA) enhance data diversity but can compromise semantic integrity due to a lack of context awareness. Paraphrasing methods, such as back-translation, generate diverse structures but rely on high-quality translation models, which can be resource-intensive and impractical for low-resource languages. A more sophisticated approach is the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to generate new data points. While it provides powerful solutions for generating coherent data, they require significant computational resources.

Despite advancements, challenges remain; EDA can introduce noise, back-translation depends on robust translation tools, and LLMs may struggle with linguistic intricacies. Additionally, token importance extraction methods, though promising, are under-explored in data augmentation contexts. This paper proposes utilizing SHAP values to swap tokens, combining targeted augmentation with efficiency for low-resource scenarios. This novel approach aims to enhance text classification tasks, improving model performance and generalization in challenging linguistic contexts while addressing the limitations of existing methods.

Regarding image classification, studies have used CNNs to enhance facial emotion detection through various methods. A study by A. Pandey and A. Gupta achieved 78.1% accuracy using CNNs with edge detection and image normalization, focusing on dataset size and feature extraction. M. S. D. Perera et al. introduced attention mechanisms for spatial data, analyzing Vision Transformers (ViTs) and achieving 80.12% accuracy, emphasizing their novelty in image work. Additionally, C. Liang and J. Dong incorporated Local Binary Patterns (LBP) into CNNs within the Convolutional Block Attention Module, reaching 71.29% accuracy by concentrating on salient facial regions for better generalization. This work advances the improvement of CNNs by employing hierarchical attention mechanisms, residual blocks, and SHAP explanations. Our approach emphasizes model interpretability, providing clear and transparent predictions, unlike the methods reviewed. This integrated strategy not only enhances accuracy but also ensures balanced performance across various emotional categories, making our model robust and applicable in real-world scenarios.

Regarding audio classification, speech and audio-based emotion recognition has advanced through audio features, data augmentation, and deep learning models. Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients’ (MFCCs), which capture the timbral characteristics of speech, have been widely used. Studies show that combining multiple features, such as spectral centroid and spectral contrast, provides a more comprehensive representation of emotional speech. Our work introduces a hybrid CNN-LSTM model, combining the spatial feature extraction capabilities of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) with the sequential learning strengths of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. This hybrid architecture effectively captures both the phonetic content and temporal dynamics of speech, leading to better emotion recognition performance. The combination of multi-feature extraction, augmentation, and hybrid model results in more accurate and robust emotion classification using speech from audio-based data.

P. J Jayaraj and colleagues in the cited research describe an application that uses brain wave analysis to identify stress levels and helps reduce stress by including audio waves, such as music. First, the application analyzes the user's brain waves to detect tension. Then, binaural beats and solfeggio frequency help reduce the level of stress that has been detected. Although the application technique works, its usefulness for relieving stress is limited to music and audio waves, which somewhat restricts its functionality in this regard. In contrast, our study adopts a multifaceted approach, identifying emotions through facial dynamics, audio frequency interactions, and text classification. These predictions are combined and fed into an activity suggestion system utilizing reinforcement learning, offering users personalized recommendations for stress-relieving activities.

With regards to the recommendation system, Kalansooriya et al. developed a social media application, "xīnlĭ," aimed at improving mental health through a machine learning-based multimodal architecture that recognizes seven basic emotions. Their system uses LSTM and CNN algorithms to analyze user video logs and suggests activities via a Q-learning recommendation engine, requiring users to submit videos regularly. Our recommendation system (RS) enhances their approach by employing Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) techniques for improved scalability, utilizing a Deep Q-Network (DQN) to approximate the action-value function. This approach also focuses on creating an optimal reward policy, addressing an open research area where existing RL algorithms fall short. Our hybrid model integrates Online Learning (OL) through the Online Passive-Aggressive Algorithm (OPA) with the DQN, enabling real-time data manipulation and facilitating high scalability. This approach aligns with findings from C. Dang et al., which suggest that combining multiple machine learning techniques yields better accuracy, and we aim to harness the strengths of various models for a more effective system.

METHODOLOGY

Text-Based Emotion Classification

  • Introduced Saliency-based Token Swapping (SSwap), a data augmentation technique using SHAP values to determine token importance for enhancing text classification.
  • SSwap identifies key tokens and swaps them based on sentence label similarity, balancing semantic integrity and data diversity.
  • Tested on GoEmotions (English) and a Sinhala dataset to validate effectiveness in low-resource languages, demonstrating improved performance compared to standard techniques like Easy Data Augmentation and Back-Translation.

Image-Based Emotion Classification

  • Addressed visual challenges in facial emotion detection using data augmentation techniques (e.g., rotation, translation, brightness adjustment) to improve generalization.
  • Enhanced CNN model with hierarchical attention mechanisms and SHAP explanations for interpretability, enabling better handling of complex conditions such as partial occlusion and varying facial angles.
  • Integrated a methodology to generalize emotion detection across video streams by analyzing cumulative emotion scores over time.

Audio-Based Emotion Classification

  • Focused on preprocessing and feature extraction from audio clips, using Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) and other spectral features for robust emotion classification.
  • Applied data augmentation techniques like time-stretching and noise addition to increase dataset diversity and model adaptability.
  • Developed a hybrid CNN-LSTM model to combine spatial and temporal features, providing a comprehensive analysis of emotional speech.

Personalized Recommendation System

  • Built a recommendation system combining Deep Q-Networks (DQN) and Online Passive-Aggressive Algorithm (OPA) to offer real-time, scalable activity suggestions based on user emotions.
  • Designed a synthetic dataset validated by psychologists, enabling the system to suggest tailored activities (e.g., hobbies, socializing) based on user profiles and feedback.
  • The system's recommendations are delivered via Gemma, a generative AI providing contextually detailed explanations, enhancing user trust and engagement.
RESEARCH PROBLEM

Emotion results from a person’s physiological, cognitive, and behavioral reactions to stimuli, profoundly impacting daily life. Continuous emotional criticism can lead individuals to lose their sense of purpose, resulting in expressions of anger through violence, fighting, or even suicide. These behaviors often stem from overwhelming negative emotions, focusing on the people of Sri Lanka, who face significant challenges due to ongoing economic turmoil. A survey highlights that economic imbalance is a primary cause of these distressing feelings, leading to mental health issues such as burnout, decreased productivity, and increased emotional distress.

Various research indicates that negative emotions can severely affect mental health, as noted in a study emphasizing the detrimental impact of frequent exposure to stress-related feelings. Different methods for detecting negative emotions, including audio analysis, text and speech analysis, and facial expression recognition, have been explored. However, these approaches often focus on specific physiological aspects and lack a comprehensive methodology suitable for the Sri Lankan context.

To address these challenges, proactive interventions and tailored mood management techniques are increasingly necessary. Machine learning approaches show promise in providing real-time insights and systematic physiological data analysis, aiding individuals in managing their stress. Studies discuss the application of machine learning in controlling negative emotions in workplaces and educational settings, which could be beneficial in the unique context of Sri Lanka’s economy.

Main Objective

Identifying emotional state through Audio, Visual and Text Data and Recommending personalized activities for middle-class demographics to improve mental well-being.

Sub Objectives

  • Accurately identifying emotions from facial images under challenging conditions
  • Expand data and address class imbalance in low-resource environments to identify emotions based on text
  • Identifying emotions based on human voice by analyzing multiple audio features
  • Provide a personalized recommendation to overcome the reason for being in this current emotional state
